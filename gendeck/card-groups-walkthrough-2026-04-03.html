1
The Plan — Planner tags groups
▸

The planner decides which cards should look identical and assigns them a shared group tag. The first card in the group is the exemplar; the rest are clones.

Plan JSON (excerpt)
{
  "cards": [
    {
      "cardIndex": 1,
      "title": "Clean Energy Investment Surge",
      "intent": "hero-stat",
      "group": "hero-stat",
      "content_guidance": "Big number: $2.2T total clean energy investment"
    },
    {
      "cardIndex": 2,
      "title": "Solar & Wind Dominance",
      "intent": "hero-stat",
      "group": "hero-stat",
      "content_guidance": "Big number: $450B in solar/wind alone"
    },
    {
      "cardIndex": 3,
      "title": "Return Multiplier",
      "intent": "hero-stat",
      "group": "hero-stat",
      "content_guidance": "Big number: 5x ROI vs fossil fuels"
    }
  ]
}

Card 1 is the exemplar. Cards 2 and 3 will clone its resolved layout.

2
Critical
The Outline — Consistent content shapes
▸
Content shapes within a group MUST be identical

Every card in a group must have the same number and type of elements. The clone step does a 1:1 content swap. If shapes differ, the clone either breaks or produces a card where text overflows / underflows the layout.

Good: Consistent shapes

Card 1 (exemplar)
<h2>$2.2T</h2>
<p>Total clean energy investment in 2023</p>
Card 2 (clone)
<h2>$450B</h2>
<p>Invested in solar and wind alone</p>
Card 3 (clone)
<h2>5×</h2>
<p>Return on investment vs fossil fuels</p>

All three: one <h2>, one <p>. Same shape. Clone will work perfectly.

Bad: Inconsistent shapes

Card 1
<h2>$2.2T</h2>
<p>Total clean energy investment in 2023</p>
Card 2
<h2>$450B</h2>
<p>Invested in solar and wind alone</p>
Card 3 ✗ breaks clone
<h2>5×</h2>
<p>Return on investment vs fossil fuels</p>
<p>Based on 10-year projections from IRENA</p>
<ul><li>Wind: 6.2x</li><li>Solar: 4.8x</li></ul>
What happens with mismatched shapes

Card 3 has extra <p> and <ul> elements. The clone step maps content by position — extra elements have no slot in the exemplar GML. They either get dropped (losing content) or overflow the layout bounds.

3
Exemplar Resolution — Full pipeline for card 1
▸

Only card 1 (the exemplar) goes through the full Structure → Layout Resolver pipeline. This is the same per-card pipeline every non-grouped card uses.

Structure (LLM call)

The structurer picks a layout variant and produces semantic GML for card 1's content.

<CARD>
  <COLUMNS layout="halves">
    <COLUMN>
      <H2>$2.2T</H2>
      <P>Total clean energy investment in 2023</P>
    </COLUMN>
    <COLUMN>
      <IMAGE query="clean energy solar panels" />
    </COLUMN>
  </COLUMNS>
</CARD>
Layout Resolver (LLM call)

The resolver adds precise spatial attributes — font sizes, alignment, padding, vertical-align.

<CARD padding="40">
  <COLUMNS layout="halves" vertical-align="center">
    <COLUMN>
      <H2 font-size="72" font-weight="800"
          color="accent">$2.2T</H2>
      <P font-size="18" color="muted"
         margin-top="8">Total clean energy
         investment in 2023</P>
    </COLUMN>
    <COLUMN>
      <IMAGE query="clean energy solar panels"
             aspect-ratio="1" border-radius="12" />
    </COLUMN>
  </COLUMNS>
</CARD>

This resolved GML becomes the template that cards 2 and 3 clone from.

4

What changed vs what stayed

Changed (purple text): Text content inside H2, P, and IMAGE query — the actual data.

Unchanged: COLUMNS layout, vertical-align, font-size, font-weight, color, padding, margin-top, aspect-ratio, border-radius — everything structural.

5
The Result — Guaranteed consistency
▸

All three cards share the exact same GML skeleton. They render with identical layouts — same column widths, same font hierarchy, same spacing.

Card 1
COLUMNS halves | v-align center
  COL: H2 72px "$2.2T"
       P  18px "Total clean energy..."
  COL: IMAGE 1:1 r12
Card 2
COLUMNS halves | v-align center
  COL: H2 72px "$450B"
       P  18px "Invested in solar..."
  COL: IMAGE 1:1 r12
Card 3
COLUMNS halves | v-align center
  COL: H2 72px "5×"
       P  18px "Return on investment..."
  COL: IMAGE 1:1 r12
Guarantee

These cards are guaranteed consistent because they share the same GML skeleton. No variance from independent LLM calls. No risk of one card picking a different layout variant, font size, or alignment strategy.

6
Cost Comparison
▸
Without Groups
Structure calls 3
Resolver calls 3
Clone operations 0
Total LLM calls 6

Each card resolved independently. No consistency guarantee — each LLM call may pick different layout variants.

With Groups
Structure calls 1
Resolver calls 1
Clone operations 2 (cheap)
Total LLM calls 2

One exemplar through the full pipeline, clones are pure string operations. Consistent by construction.

67% fewer LLM calls + guaranteed visual consistency
Connection to template remix: Card groups produce templates on the fly — the exemplar's resolved GML acts as a one-off template. Template remix starts with pre-made templates. Same mechanism: take a GML skeleton, swap content in, skip structure + resolver.
